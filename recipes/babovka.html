Babovka recipe

Here in the Czech Republic, a bábovka cake belongs to the most popular desserts made at weekends. Marble bundt cake means mramorová bábovka in Czech. When I was little, my dad usually made the bábovka. He prepared this lovely swirl cake the same way as in this recipe: vanilla part of bábovka generously marbled with cocoa batter. We were always curious about the two-color pattern inside the bábovka once we cut it open.

Ingredients

  • greek yogurt
  • all-purpose flour
  • granulated sugar
  • eggs
  • baking powder
  • vegetable oil
  • cocoa powder
  • milk
  • vanilla extract

Easy Steps

  1. Beat eggs and sugar in a mixing bowl for 5 minutes. Use a hand-held mixer set to the highest rotations.

  2. Add vegetable oil, yogurt, vanilla, flour mixed with baking powder and continue mixing for another 2 minutes.

  3. Mix a third of the batter with cocoa powder and a little milk.

  4. Pour half of the white vanilla batter into the bundt cake pan, add cocoa batter, finish with the white batter layer.

  5. Bake in a pre-heated oven for 50 minutes.

    6. Once the bábovka cools down, you just dust it with powdered sugar.
